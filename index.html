Marrakech, Morocco

Marrakech is one of those places that completely hijacks your senses the second you arrive. It is often called the "Ocre City" because of the warm red sandstone walls that seem to glow when the sun begins to set. The real magic is inside the old Medina, a maze of narrow streets filled with colourful markets, the smell of spices, and talented artisans creating handmade goods. One of my favourite experiences was exploring the souks and discovering hidden corners of the city. At night, Jemaa el-Fnaa square comes alive with food stalls, musicians, storytellers, and performers, creating an atmosphere unlike anywhere else. I also enjoyed visiting the peaceful Majorelle Garden, which provided a calm escape from the busy streets. The combination of history, culture, architecture, and energy makes Marrakech a truly unforgettable city that I would love to visit again.
